How to become a CPA start from high school

Introduction

So first, what's a CPA?
It stands for Certified Public Accountant. CPAs play key roles within diverse segments of the economy including industry, public accounting, government, education and the not-for-profit sector.

Second: Complete all the prerequisite courses in your College/University

The CPA preparatory courses are high-quality educational courses accessible for students wanting to complete the prerequisite education required to enter the CPA Professional Education Program (CPA PEP). The CPA preparatory courses are competency-based, emphasizing development of the key areas of knowledge and enabling competencies required of accountants and senior financial managers.

Third: Work hard on your CPA PEP and pass the CFE

You have to register to complete the courses and exams of the CPA Professional Education Program (CPA PEP) first. After completing the CPA PEP, take the Common Final Examination(CFE) and pass it.

The Admission Requirements to be admitted to the CPA PEP:
        • an undergraduate degree or master’s degree
        • completion of prerequisite learning
        • 120 credit-hours or equivalent of education (30 credit-hours1/academic year)
        • good character

1 Credit-hour is an input measure meant to reflect student time required to earn credit for the course. Generally, a three-credit course requires 33 hours of contact time (lecture, and so on) or 132 hours of student engagement time (lectures, tutorial, practice activities, testing or assessment, or preparation).

Fourth: Accumulate work experience for a period of time

After pass the CFE, a total of 30 months of internship or formal work experience is required to complete the Practical Experience Requirements(CPA PER).

There are two routes you can take:
- CPA pre-approved programs: in which future CPAs gain relevant experience in training positions pre-approved by the profession.
- Experience verification: in which future CPAs demonstrate relevant experience at an employer of their choice.

There are five common elements that support both routes:
        • You gain relevant experience and develop as a professional accountant in a minimum of 30 months.
        • Your experience must be appropriately supervised.
        • You must record detailed reports at regular intervals.
        • You must meet and discuss your progress at least semi-annually with a CPA mentor.
        • Your experience is assessed by the CPA profession.

It will take you at least two years to become a CPA and part of the reason is because this part takes much more time to complete.
