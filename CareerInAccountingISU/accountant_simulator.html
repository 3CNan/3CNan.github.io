Coming to next step, to finish the CPA certification program, you still need one more thing……

-- the CPA PER(Practical Experience Requirements).

You have two routes to obtain your practical experience requirements:

• CPA pre-approved programs:
in which future CPAs gain relevant experience in training positions pre-approved by the profession.

• Experience verification:
in which future CPAs demonstrate relevant experience at an employer of their choice. (END)

You can gain experience from either one of them or from the combination of both, but there are 5 common requirements:

1. Qualifying Experience

(Both routes take a minimum of 30 months; this includes an allowance of up to 20 weeks of time away from work (including vacation time). You have a CPA mentor to start.)

2. Appropriate Supervision

(Your supervisor is responsible for assigning you the required experience in a pre-approved program verifying that your experience is factually accurate in your experience report.)

3. Reporting & Self-Assessment

(The experience report details the duration of the experience, the type of experience being gained and your self-assessment of this experience. Do it at least twice a year and discussed with your CPA mentor.)

4. CPA Mentorship

(You will meet with your CPA mentor at least semi-annually to review your progress against set required competencies.)

5. Review by Profession

(At key milestones, the experience report must be reviewed by your provincial/regional body.) (END)

You complete the CPA PEP and CPA PER, and successfully pass the CFE(Common Final Examination).

Now you are already a CPA!.
